Case Studies

Insights from our work with destinations and hospitality businesses worldwide.

Caribbean Destination Recovery

After a devastating hurricane season, we worked with three island nations to rebuild their tourism offerings. Our market analysis revealed an opportunity to reposition these destinations as "authentic Caribbean" alternatives to more developed islands.

Key insight: Disaster recovery can be a catalyst for strategic repositioning when approached holistically.

African Safari Lodge Turnaround

A luxury safari lodge was struggling with low occupancy despite excellent reviews. Our assessment identified that their marketing was targeting the wrong demographic - adventure seekers rather than luxury travelers seeking comfort in the wilderness.

Key insight: Product quality doesn't guarantee market fit - alignment between product and marketing is essential.

Urban Hotel Repositioning

A 1980s convention hotel in a major US city was losing market share to newer properties. Rather than competing on amenities, we helped them reposition as the "best connected" hotel for business travelers, leveraging their central location and existing transit links.

Key insight: Sometimes operational strengths can become marketing advantages when framed correctly.

Asian Cultural Tourism Development

Working with a regional government, we identified that their cultural assets were being underutilized by focusing only on international tourists. By developing domestic tourism programs first, they created authentic experiences that later became unique selling points for international visitors.

Key insight: Domestic tourism can be the foundation for successful international tourism development.

Cruise Port Destination Strategy

A Caribbean port was receiving cruise visitors but seeing minimal economic benefit. We developed a "downtown as attraction" strategy that encouraged passengers to explore beyond the port area, increasing visitor spending by 320% within two years.

Key insight: Cruise tourism benefits require intentional design of the passenger experience beyond the dock.

Boutique Hotel Brand Expansion

A successful boutique hotel wanted to expand but risked losing its unique character. We helped them develop a franchise model that maintained design standards while allowing local adaptations, resulting in 12 new properties that each reflected their location while maintaining brand consistency.

Key insight: Scalability requires balancing standardization with local relevance.
